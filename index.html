        <p class="kicker">Step-by-step</p>

        <h2>From Dew Rookie to Meadow Master</h2>

        <ol class="steps" id="stepList">

          <li>

            <strong>Scout your turf.</strong>

            <p>Find soft, even grass free of hazards. Your lawn, a park, or a field with permission. Bonus points for morning dew—nature’s lane lubricant.</p>

          </li>

          <li>

            <strong>Warm up the joints.</strong>

            <p>Neck rolls, shoulder circles, hip hinges. Think <em>mobility first</em>—you’ll glide better when your body’s a bit springy.</p>

          </li>

          <li>

            <strong>Choose your stroke.</strong>

            <p><em>Freelawn</em> (freestyle), <em>breastbloom</em> (breaststroke), or the advanced <em>butterbur</em> (butterfly). Start slow; respect the shrub.</p>

          </li>

          <li>

            <strong>Low drag posture.</strong>

            <p>Hinge slightly, core tight, chin neutral. Imagine the grass is thick water—you’re slicing, not stomping.</p>

          </li>

          <li>

            <strong>Kicks, not steps.</strong>

            <p>Brush the toes through the blades in small, quick kicks. Quiet feet = smooth flow. No lawnmower energy.</p>

          </li>

          <li>

            <strong>Pull and recover.</strong>

            <p>Sweep hands just above the grass, then recover forward. Keep wrists relaxed so blades can part gracefully.</p>

          </li>

          <li>

            <strong>Breathe with the breeze.</strong>

            <p>Inhale when the wind (or your turn) faces away; exhale when you dip towards the stems. Sync breath to rhythm.</p>

          </li>

          <li>

            <strong>Finish with a glide.</strong>

            <p>After each pull, float the arms to feel momentum. Celebrate with a victory roll in clover.</p>

          </li>

        </ol>

          <p class="kicker">Form cues</p>

          <h3>Technique Checklist</h3>

          <ul>

            <li>Head still; eyes soft-focus 2–3 meters ahead.</li>

            <li>Shoulders down & back—no shrugging into the weeds.</li>

            <li>Elbows high on the catch; the grass will part.</li>

            <li>Light toe flicks; keep ankles floppy.</li>

            <li>Exhale slowly through pursed lips; smile optional but recommended.</li>

          </ul>

        </div>

        <div class="card">

          <p class="kicker">Common oops</p>

          <h3>Lawn-Order Violations</h3>

          <ul>

            <li>Marching. If you hear thuds, you’re walking—not swimming.</li>

            <li>Rigid wrists creating clumps. Stay fluid.</li>

            <li>Holding breath. The meadow is forgiving; your lungs should be too.</li>
